# Phonics Pals & Guided Reading — Video Tutorial Script

A ready-to-record walkthrough for teachers and students. Follow the steps on
screen while reading the **Say** lines aloud. Total run time ≈ 8–10 minutes.

**How to record (Mac):** open QuickTime Player → File → New Screen Recording →
record the browser window at `https://phonics.test/`. Speak the **Say** lines as
you do each **Do** step.

---

## Scene 0 — Welcome (about 40 sec)

**Do:** Show the home screen with all the coloured tool cards.

**Say:**
> "Welcome to Phonics Pals and Guided Reading. This app helps children learn to
> read step by step — first the letter sounds, then blending sounds into words,
> then spelling, then reading whole stories out loud. There are nine tools, and
> in this video I'll show you what each one does and when to use it."

**Do:** Point out that every card has a name and a little description under it.

---

## Scene 1 — Signing in (about 20 sec)

**Do:** Show the sign-in screen. Enter the staff email and shared password.

**Say:**
> "First, sign in with your staff email and the shared password. This keeps the
> app just for our class."

---

## Scene 2 — Phonics 🙆 (about 60 sec)

*What it's for: learning the single letter sounds with an action for each, then
blending them.*

**Do:** Open **Phonics**. Tap a few letters to hear the pure sound. Show the
action/picture for a sound. Demonstrate blending a short word.

**Say:**
> "Phonics is where we start. Tap any letter to hear its true sound — not the
> letter name, just the sound, said firmly. Each sound has an action to help the
> children remember it. Once they know a few sounds, we can blend them together
> into a word."

---

## Scene 3 — Sound It Out 🔤 (about 45 sec)

*What it's for: type ANY word and hear it broken into sounds and blended.*

**Do:** Open **Sound It Out**. Type a simple word (e.g. `ship`). Play it
sound-by-sound, then blended.

**Say:**
> "Sound It Out lets you type any word at all. The app breaks it into its sounds
> and blends it back together, so children can practise a word from their book
> or their name. Great for tricky words that come up during the day."

---

## Scene 4 — Flashcards 🎴 (about 60 sec)

*What it's for: flip cards to self-check — Deck 1 letter sounds, Deck 2 blending
words. Tap the picture or the word to hear it.*

**Do:** Open **Flashcards**. Show **Letter Sounds** deck: flip a card, tap the
**picture** and the **word** to hear them, use **🔊 Hear sound** for the letter.
Then show **Blend Words** deck: tap the picture/word to hear the word, use
**🔉 Blend it!**. Show **Print / Download** for paper cards.

**Say:**
> "Flashcards are for quick self-checking. In the Letter Sounds deck, see the
> letter, say the sound, then flip for a picture — and you can tap the picture or
> the word to hear it. In the Blend Words deck, see the picture and blend the
> sounds into a word. You can also print any deck as double-sided cards."

---

## Scene 5 — Letter Formation ✍️ (about 45 sec)

*What it's for: learn how to write each letter with the correct stroke.*

**Do:** Open **Letter Formation**. Pick a letter, play the stroke animation,
trace it.

**Say:**
> "Letter Formation shows how to write each letter the right way, with the
> correct starting point and stroke order. Children watch the animation, then
> trace along to build good handwriting habits."

---

## Scene 6 — Spelling 🔡 (about 45 sec)

*What it's for: hear a word, break it into sounds, and spell it.*

**Do:** Open **Spelling**. Play a word, segment it into sounds, build the
spelling.

**Say:**
> "Spelling is blending in reverse. The child hears a word, breaks it into its
> sounds, and chooses the letters to spell it. This links the sounds they know to
> the letters that make them."

---

## Scene 7 — Tricky Words 🌟 (about 40 sec)

*What it's for: practising sight words that can't be sounded out (the, said,
was…).*

**Do:** Open **Tricky Words**. Show a few high-frequency words being practised.

**Say:**
> "Some words can't be sounded out — like 'the', 'said', and 'was'. We call these
> tricky words, and children need to recognise them on sight. This tool gives
> them focused practice on exactly those words."

---

## Scene 8 — Sentences & Stories 📚 (about 50 sec)

*What it's for: reading leveled stories and sentences, matched to reading levels.*

**Do:** Open **Sentences & Stories**. Pick a level, open a story, read a page,
tap a word to hear it.

**Say:**
> "Sentences and Stories brings it all together into real reading. Choose a level
> from Year 1 to Year 6, open a story, and read. Tap any word you're unsure of to
> hear it. It's a gentle, no-pressure way to practise reading connected text."

---

## Scene 9 — Guided Reading 🎤 (about 80 sec)

*What it's for: reading a story aloud, getting an accuracy/fluency report, a word
coach, and 10 optional comprehension questions per story.*

**Do:** Open **Guided Reading**. Pick a level and a story. Read it aloud (mic
listens). Show the **report** (stars, accuracy, words-per-minute, words to
practise). Open **Practice with Coach**. Then open **"💬 Talk about the story ·
10 questions"** and show the mix of the tap-to-answer question and the discussion
questions.

**Say:**
> "Guided Reading is our read-aloud tool. The child reads a story out loud and
> the app listens, then gives a report — a star rating, reading accuracy, words
> per minute, and a list of words to practise. There's a Coach to drill those
> tricky words. And after reading, there are ten comprehension questions for each
> story — a mix of a tap-to-answer question and talking questions about the story.
> These are completely optional; they're just for discussion, and nothing is
> marked right or wrong."

---

## Scene 10 — Reading Assessment 📋 (about 90 sec)

*What it's for: a two-stage placement check that gives a reading level.*

**Do:** Open **Reading Assessment**. Enter a student's name and Start.
- **Stage 1 (Word check):** the child reads single words aloud; the teacher marks
  each **✓ Got it** or **✗ Not yet**. Point out the **"Words read wrongly"** box.
  Explain it **stops after 2 words wrong**, then suggests a reading level (a
  Lexile, e.g. 247L).
- **Stage 2:** read a story at that level. Show the report — the reading level,
  the score, and the move-up / stay / move-down recommendation.
- Show **Print / Download** for the one-page report.

**Say:**
> "The Reading Assessment places a child at the right reading level. In Stage One,
> the child reads single words from easy to hard, and you mark each one correct or
> not. After two words read wrongly, it stops and suggests a reading level based on
> the last word they read well. In Stage Two, they read a short story at that level.
> Only if they read it well does the app suggest moving up to the next level. At the
> end you get a printable one-page report you can keep or send home."

---

## Scene 11 — Closing (about 30 sec)

**Do:** Return to the home screen showing all the tools.

**Say:**
> "So that's Phonics Pals and Guided Reading: sounds and blending in Phonics and
> Sound It Out, practice in Flashcards, Letter Formation, Spelling and Tricky
> Words, real reading in Stories and Guided Reading, and a placement check in the
> Reading Assessment. Start with the sounds and work your way up. Happy reading!"

---

### Suggested teaching order (for the video or a poster)

1. **Phonics** — learn the sounds
2. **Sound It Out** — blend any word
3. **Flashcards** — self-check sounds & words
4. **Letter Formation** — write the letters
5. **Spelling** — spell from sounds
6. **Tricky Words** — sight words
7. **Sentences & Stories** — read leveled texts
8. **Guided Reading** — read aloud + comprehension
9. **Reading Assessment** — check the reading level
